Safety: open source and official releases

  • Open source: yt-dlp is audited by a large community.
  • Official source: We link to the official GitHub release only to avoid tampered binaries.
  • No bloat: No installers, no telemetry, no background services.

Legality: what you should know

  • Copyright: Do not download copyrighted material without permission.
  • Terms of Service: Many websites restrict downloading; violating ToS can have consequences regardless of local copyright law.
  • Jurisdiction: Laws differ; consult local rules and, if needed, a legal professional.

Typically allowed content

  • Your own uploads and backups
  • Public domain works
  • Content under licenses that allow download/reuse (e.g., certain Creative Commons)

Best practices checklist

  • Use official downloads (GitHub release)
  • Keep yt-dlp updated: yt-dlp -U
  • Install FFmpeg for merges and audio conversions
  • Respect copyright and ToS; download only when you have rights
  • Avoid re-sharing content without permission

Privacy notes

  • yt-dlp does not include adware or telemetry
  • When using cookies/auth, protect your accounts and remove exported cookies when done
  • Check our Privacy Policy for site analytics details

FAQ

Is it legal to download for personal use?

This depends on your jurisdiction and the site's Terms. Some licenses allow personal copies; others prohibit downloading entirely. Verify before proceeding.

Can I use downloads commercially?

Only if the license explicitly permits it or you have permission from the rights holder.

Why does SmartScreen appear?

Portable open-source .exe files without installers can trigger SmartScreen. Use More info → Run anyway if you trust the official GitHub source.
